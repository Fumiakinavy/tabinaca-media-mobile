## Quick Overview

Hop between tiny meat-specialty eateries packed into Chitose Kaikan's 2F & 3F in Center-gai. There are ~28 micro-restaurants; grab a seat or stand at 2–3 counters and order a signature bite and a drink at each. Most places are casual, loud, and fun; many have picture/EN menus.

## What You'll Do

- **Start at the lantern entrance** of Chitose Kaikan (Niku Yokocho) and pick your first counter
- **Order 1 small plate** (yakitori, yakiniku bite, beef sushi, etc.) + 1 drink. Move on
- **Repeat at 1–2 more spots**; finish with a shared photo under the alley signs
- **Tip**: many shops post best-sellers up front—point to order if needed

## What's Included

- Self-guided route & venue map (digital)
- English order prompts you can print ("1 beer, please / chef's recommendation")
- On-site staff provide basic EN support at some counters (varies by shop)

## Perfect For

- Small groups or couples wanting a lively, "only-in-Tokyo" food dash without a guide

## Need-to-Know

- **Budget**: ¥2,000–¥4,000 per person for 2–3 plates + a drink
- **Peak hours**: 19:00–22:00; go earlier for easier seating
- **Walk-in culture**: each stall has its own bill & hours
- **Location**: Chitose Kaikan 2F–3F, 13-8 Udagawachō, Shibuya (5 min from Hachiko/Center-gai)
- **Scale**: about 28 meat-focused mini-restaurants inside
