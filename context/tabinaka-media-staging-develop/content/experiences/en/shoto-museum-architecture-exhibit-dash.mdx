## Quick Overview

Step off Shibuya's buzz into a serene, architect-lovers' art stop. In 30–60 minutes you'll: admire Seiichi Shirai's iconic stone façade and light-filled atrium, view the current special exhibition, and snap allowed photos of the striking interior. Easy walk from Shibuya (15 min) or Shinsen (5 min). Exhibition hours 10:00–18:00, Fridays until 20:00 (during exhibition periods). Admission varies by show.

## What You'll Do

- **Walk the exterior**: spot the curved roofline and pink-tinted kounseki stone blocks before heading inside
- **Glide through the circular atrium**: look up for the oculus and vertical fins—this is the museum's signature photo moment (no flash/tripods)
- **See the current special exhibition** (rotates through modern & contemporary themes). If you're visiting now, check the museum site for exact dates and highlights
- **Take permitted photos** for personal use, then exit via the quiet Shoto backstreets—perfect reset between Shibuya stops

## What's Included

- Self-paced route (exterior → atrium → galleries)
- On-site signage (JP/EN varies by show) and gallery handouts (when provided)
- Museum cloak lockers (¥100 refundable) for light bags

## Perfect For

- Architecture fans who want a quick "wow" space in Shibuya
- Travelers building a mini museum crawl between shopping and cafés
- Photographers hunting geometric interiors (within house rules)

## Need-to-Know

- **Hours**: 10:00–18:00 (Fri until 20:00 during exhibitions); last entry 30 min before close. Closed between shows and after national holidays; check the schedule
- **Tickets**: Vary by exhibition (Shibuya City residents free on Fridays; kids free on weekends/holidays). Buy on site
- **Photography**: Allowed unless marked; no flash, video, tripods, or selfie sticks; avoid shooting from the 1F corridor into rooms; be mindful of other visitors
- **Access**: 5-min walk from Shinsen (Keio Inokashira) or ~15-min from Shibuya Station. Address: 2-14-14 Shoto, Shibuya
- **Architect trivia**: Building by Seiichi Shirai; two floors above ground, two below, centered on a daylight atrium
