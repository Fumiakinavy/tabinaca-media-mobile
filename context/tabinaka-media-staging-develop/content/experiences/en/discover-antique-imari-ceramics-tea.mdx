## Quick Overview

Explore the special exhibition “The Colors of Old Imari: Glazes” (July 11–September 28) at the Toguri Museum of Art, home to a world-class collection of Hizen porcelain, Chinese and Korean ceramics. Finish your visit with a leisurely tea and wagashi in the museum café.

## What You’ll Do

- **Step 1**: View the “The Colors of Old Imari: Glazes” special exhibition, admiring antique Imari and Nabeshima wares.
- **Step 2**: Stroll through the museum’s permanent galleries featuring ceramics collected by founder Toru Toguri.
- **Step 3**: Head to the on-site café to savor traditional Japanese sweets (wagashi) and freshly whisked matcha tea.

## What's Included

- Admission to the special exhibition “The Colors of Old Imari: Glazes”
- One serving of wagashi and matcha tea at the café

## Perfect For

- Ceramic and art enthusiasts eager to study antique glazes
- Culture seekers looking to delve into Japanese craft heritage
- Visitors wanting a calm, reflective gallery stroll
- Tea lovers craving an authentic matcha & wagashi pairing
