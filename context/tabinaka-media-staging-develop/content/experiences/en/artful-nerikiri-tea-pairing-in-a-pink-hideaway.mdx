## Quick Overview

Tucked between **Yoyogi-Uehara** and **Shibuya's Okushibu** area, **Kantan-na Yume** is a minimalist, pink-toned hideaway where **traditional wagashi (Japanese sweets)** meet **modern design and flavor**.  
Enjoy handmade **nerikiri** with unexpected twists like *lemon cream cheese* or *matcha chocolate*, paired with **Uji matcha**, **Japanese tea**, or even **tea cocktails** on select days.

## What You'll Do

- **Step 1:** Choose the **3-item tasting set** (1 nerikiri + 2 sweets) from a seasonal selection (~¥1,300)  
- **Step 2:** Pair with your choice of **matcha or Japanese tea**; opt for **tea + gin/whisky cocktails** when available  
- **Step 3:** Learn the story of each sweet from the English-friendly menu or staff explanation  
- **Step 4:** Relax in the **tatami-meets-modern** interior and enjoy the quiet, artful vibe  
- **Step 5:** Snap a photo—the pastel colors and ceramics make every plate *Instagram gold*  

## What's Included

- Self-guided tasting with **staff pointers on flavors and pairings**  
- Access to a **calm, two-floor café** with tatami-inspired seating  
- **Takeout option** available—perfect for enjoying wagashi in Yoyogi Park  

## Perfect For

- **Dessert lovers** craving an artistic, light, and modern Japanese sweet experience  
- **Couples or friends** looking for a stylish, quiet afternoon break  
- **Travelers** who want to experience wagashi craftsmanship without a full workshop  

## Need-to-Know

- **Hours:** 12:00–18:30 (L.O. 18:00); closed Mondays (check Instagram for changes)  
- **Address:** 41-3 Kamiyamacho, Shibuya-ku (8–10 min from Yoyogi-Hachiman / Yoyogi-Koen)  
- **Reservations:** DM via Instagram often accepted; walk-ins until sold out  
- **Budget:** ¥1,300–2,000 per person (depends on drink/pairing choice)  
- **Atmosphere:** Quiet, design-forward, ideal for a mindful dessert stop
