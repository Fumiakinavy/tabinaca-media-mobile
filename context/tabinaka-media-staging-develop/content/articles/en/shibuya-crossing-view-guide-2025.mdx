### So, Why Is This Intersection So Famous Anyway?

Before we dive into _where_ to see it, let's quickly cover _why_ you're here. What makes this specific intersection a global icon?

- **The Sheer Scale:** Widely considered the world's busiest pedestrian crossing. At peak times, up to 3,000 people flood the intersection every two minutes; on a busy day, as many as 500,000 may cross it.
- **The Cultural Epicenter:** A gateway to global fashion trends, art, music, and food. Featured in films like _Lost in Translation_ and _The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift_.
- **The Loyal Dog:** Beside the crossing stands the bronze statue of Hachiko, the Akita who waited daily for his owner for nearly 10 years after his passing. A beloved Tokyo meeting spot and symbol of loyalty.

### 1. SHIBUYA SKY: The "Wow" Factor

- **Vibe:** Modern, breathtaking, full-on experience
- **How to Get There:** Connected to Shibuya Station—follow signs for Scramble Square, then elevator to the 14th-floor entrance
- **Cost:** ¥2,200 online (¥2,500 at counter); varied rates for students/children. Book timed-entry online—sunset slots sell out fast.
- **Insider Tip:** Store loose items (¥100 refundable locker) before heading to the rooftop. Only phones and strap-secured cameras allowed.

---

### 2. Magnet by SHIBUYA109: The Open-Air Rooftop

- **Vibe:** Casual, trendy, often less crowded
- **How to Get There:** Enter Magnet by SHIBUYA109 (corner of the crossing), take elevator to rooftop (RF)
- **Cost:** ¥1,800 (includes one drink); optional "Crossing Photo" service for an extra fee
- **Insider Tip:** Plexiglass barrier ensures clear shots—best at sunset and when neon lights come alive.

---

### 3. Starbucks (SHIBUYA TSUTAYA): The Iconic Classic

- **Vibe:** Bustling, iconic—window seats feel like winning a lottery
- **How to Get There:** 2nd floor of the SHIBUYA TSUTAYA building facing Hachiko Exit
- **Cost:** Price of a coffee (tall size only to manage crowds)
- **Insider Tip:** For a calmer experience, head to the SHARE LOUNGE on 3F–4F (¥1,650/hr) for coworking-style seating, free Wi-Fi, and unlimited soft drinks.

---

### 4. Shibuya Mark City Walkway: The Free & Easy Shot

- **Vibe:** Functional commuter corridor with a million-dollar view
- **How to Get There:** From JR lines, follow signs for Keio Inokashira Line/Shibuya Mark City; walkway between JR and Inokashira entrance
- **Cost:** Free
- **Insider Tip:** Press your lens against the glass to eliminate reflections at night.

---

### 5. Shibuya Hikarie Sky Lobby: The Free Panoramic

- **Vibe:** Calm, spacious, sophisticated indoor lobby
- **How to Get There:** Pedestrian deck or underground passages to Shibuya Hikarie; elevator to 11F
- **Cost:** Free
- **Insider Tip:** Open 7:00 AM–midnight (June 2025)—perfect for post-dinner city views.

---

### 6. L'Occitane Café: The Relaxed Alternative

- **Vibe:** Chic, Provencal-inspired, elegant atmosphere
- **How to Get There:** Enter through L'Occitane store on 1F of the Shibuya Station Front Building; window seats on 2F–3F
- **Cost:** ¥2,000–¥3,000 for meal/drink set
- **Insider Tip:** Reserve dinner in advance to guarantee a prime seat at dusk.

---

### 7. Shibuya Excel Hotel Tokyu: The High-End Escape

- **Vibe:** Upscale, serene, luxury dining backdrop
- **How to Get There:** Within the Shibuya Mark City complex; take elevator to 25F
- **Cost:** Lunch ¥3,000–¥4,000; dinner ¥10,000+
- **Insider Tip:** Choose between "Shunsai" (Japanese) or "A Bientôt" (French) restaurants—reservations highly recommended.
